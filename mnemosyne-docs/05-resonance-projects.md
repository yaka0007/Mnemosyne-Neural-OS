# Resonance Projects

> *Not all memory belongs together. Your life chronicles and your coding sessions are not the same constellation.*

---

## Memory Sovereignty at the Chronicle Level

When you create a Chronicle, you choose which **Resonance Project** receives it.

This is not a folder. It is a choice about which **semantic universe** this state snapshot belongs to — which Nexus Graph it will be embedded into, which future queries will be able to surface it, which AI identity will be shaped by it.

A Chronicle saved to **Mnemosyne** (your coding project) does not appear in **Ma vie** (your personal life space) — even if both are stored locally, encrypted, sovereign.

---

## What a Resonance Project Is

A Resonance Project is a named, isolated semantic memory space. Each project has:

- Its own **Nexus Graph** — a constellation of connected files, chronicles, notes
- Its own **embedding index** — all content vectorized and stored locally
- Its own **access policies** — through the FGAC system
- Its own **AI context** — the AI's identity shifts based on which project is active

When you enter a Resonance Project and ask a question, the AI searches *that constellation* — not your entire memory archive.

---

## Why This Matters

**Example: Two projects, one person**

Tony has two Resonance Projects:
- **Mnemosyne** — everything related to the platform build: architecture decisions, code chronicles, technical breakthroughs, Irina's testimony
- **Ma vie** — personal reflections, family moments, the life journal intended for his daughter

When he's in a coding session and asks "what was the FGAC decision we made last month?" — only the Mnemosyne constellation is searched. His daughter's future reading experience is not contaminated by architecture logs.

When his daughter one day opens **Ma vie**, she navigates a constellation of her father's life states — his intentions, his emotions, his vision. Not a single line of code.

**The Chronicles go to the right universe at the moment they are created.**

---

## Project Selection in Practice

When creating a Chronicle (manual or via Auto-Chronicle configuration), the **Resonance Project** selector shows your available projects. One click assigns the Chronicle to the right constellation.

If the Auto-Chronicle is active, the last selected project is used silently — unless Silent mode is off, in which case the modal confirms before crystallizing.

---

## Multiple Projects, One Identity

Each Resonance Project can have a different **Soul** — a different AI identity configured through Soul Studio. The AI that helps you build software might be configured for precision and architecture. The AI that helps you journal your life might be configured for empathy and reflection.

Same system. Different states. Different constellations. Different voices.

This is the promise of Mnemosyne: **not one AI that knows everything about you indiscriminately, but a sovereign system where memory is organized by intention.**
