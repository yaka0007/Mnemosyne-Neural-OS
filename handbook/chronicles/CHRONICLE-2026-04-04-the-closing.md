## On Productive Quiproquos

This session produced two of them.

**The first** — SWEEP → `mnemo sweep`. The practitioner referenced his code refactoring protocol. The AI heard "ritual of synthesis" and proposed an end-of-day Chronicle command. The practitioner corrected the misread. Then kept the command. `mnemo sweep` is now part of the CLI specification.

**The second** — the Resonance tags. The practitioner asked for tags at the end of the Chronicle. The AI made them invisible (HTML comment block, machine-readable, hidden in rendering). The practitioner corrected: he wanted visible tags too. The correction produced the canonical format: visible hashtags for humans, `<!--resonance-->` block for machines. Both layers. Neither would have existed complete without the misread.

This is worth naming as a pattern: **the most precise instructions sometimes produce over-completion**. The AI completes what it inferred the instruction meant, not just what was said. When the practitioner catches the delta and corrects it, the output is often better than either the exact instruction or the inference would have produced alone.

The Veto applied to a quiproquo is not a rejection. It is a negotiation between two incomplete models of the same intent.
